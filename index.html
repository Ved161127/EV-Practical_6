Aim & Objectives

Aim: Introduction to projections of planes and drawing them using instruments and CAD software.

  • To understand and visualize the orientation of planar surfaces with respect to HP, VP and auxiliary planes through orthographic projections.
  • To construct accurate projections of various plane figures using manual drafting tools and CAD software.

Theory: Projections of Planes

A plane surface is a flat two-dimensional figure such as a triangle, square, circle, pentagon or hexagon used in engineering drawing.

Standard Positions and Views

Plane Position Front View (FV) Top View (TV)
Parallel to HP Line / Point True Shape
Parallel to VP True Shape Line / Point
Perpendicular to both HP and VP Foreshortened Shape Foreshortened Shape
Inclined to HP, perpendicular to VP Inclined, Foreshortened True Shape
Inclined to VP, perpendicular to HP True Shape Inclined, Foreshortened

General Steps for Projections

  1. Identify the orientation of the plane (e.g., inclined to VP and perpendicular to HP).
  2. Draw the true shape in the appropriate view (front or top).
  3. Project orthogonal lines to obtain the other view.
  4. Draw reference line XY between HP and VP.
  5. Label all corner points clearly (A, B, C, etc.).

Generic Drawing Procedure

  1. Select the given plane figure (triangle / circle / polygon) and note all given dimensions and angles.
  2. Decide where the true shape will appear (FV or TV) based on which plane it is resting on.
  3. Draw the true shape to scale using instruments or CAD commands.
  4. Project perpendicular lines to get the corresponding view.
  5. Apply the specified inclinations to HP or VP by rotation or auxiliary view method.
  6. Darken final visible edges and keep construction lines light.

Batch C — Exercises from Manual

Batch C — Exercise 1

An isosceles triangle ABC having its base AB = 40 mm and altitude = 60 mm is resting on the V.P. on its base AB. Draw the projections of the plane when its surface is inclined to V.P. at an angle of 45° and the base AB which is on the V.P. is making an angle of 50° to the H.P.

Step 1: Draw the XY reference line.

Step 2: Draw the true shape of triangle ABC with given dimensions on VP.

Step 3: Locate points A, B, and C and complete the triangular shape.

Step 4: Draw projectors from all points perpendicular to XY.

Step 5: Obtain the initial top view of the triangle below the XY line.

Step 6: Draw the inclination line at the given angle from point C.

Step 7: Rotate the front view of the triangle about the base to make the surface inclined.

Step 8: From the rotated view, project the new positions of A, B, and C to obtain the final top view.

Step 9: Join the points to get the final projections of the inclined triangular plane.

Exercise 2 (Batch C) – Circular Lamina

Batch C — Exercise 2

A circular lamina is inclined to HP and VP. Draw the projections of the lamina.

Step 1: Draw the XY reference line.

Step 2: Draw the top view of the cube resting on one corner in simple position.

Step 3: Project the corresponding front view.

Step 4: Rotate the cube so its body diagonal becomes parallel to HP.

Step 5: Project the new top and front views after rotation.

Step 6: Incline the top view so the body diagonal makes 45° with VP.

Step 7: Project the final front view and complete with visible and hidden edges.
